From the Better User Experience Through Storytelling Articles: How can framing your project in a story format help you refine your ideas? Write a paragraph explanation.

It really just makes it a lot easier to view what you're doing and what your plan is when you can better imagine exactly what the purpose of what you're accomplishing is and how others will respond or interact with it, and it makes sense that any project would end up turning out better if it's created with greater consideration of what people may be thinking when they see and interact with different elements of it and experience what you've made.

From Meet Your Type: A Field Guide to Love and Typography: What are different ways to establish hierarchy (or emphasis) in typography design? Provide a link to a good example of hierarchy.

There are many ways to establish hierarchy through all the ways you can alter text, like by adjusting the size of it to emphasize the first words you want someone to look at like titles or subheadings. The same effect can be achieved by changing the color of text or italicizing it or making it bold, all of the above will draw attention especially when seen in contrast with simpler more basic text. I think a good example of this being done is the Etsy website, where the name of the site is bright orange and there are various headings for things that use different font sizes and have some in bold, as well as having certain bits of text in gray so the black text they're next to will catch your attention first.

Creative Brief



The problem I’d like to address is that I don’t have a good way of recording what tea I drink, keeping track of how much I drink, and easily categorizing what types I drink. I’ve been wanting to log the tea I drink for quite some time and do so in a way where I can try to include information like caffeine content, the company the tea is from, exactly how much I had, what ingredients may be in it, and more things like that. It would really help me keep track of what my caffeine intake looks like, what my favorite teas seem to be over the course of weeks or months, and it would make it easier for me to recommend specific teas to friends by being able to show them more exact details on the teas. My audience is really just myself and friends/family I’d say, because I’m the one who will be benefitting most from keeping a clear record of the tea I drink but certain friends and family members can also benefit by being able to look at what I’ve recorded and get recommendations for themselves from it. I suppose my competition would be other apps and sites that may have tea logging available, but I wouldn’t consider them real competition for me specifically because I want to log more in depth info and specific things that these apps and sites don’t seem to have as options. This is something I’ve wanted to do for a while, but it would’ve been less efficient doing it how I originally might've ended up doing it which is by using a google doc, which would be much less pretty and organized looking than an actual website and wouldn't allow me to add as many features. I'll consider this project a success if it ends up serving its purpose as it should and allows me to log tea how I want while also being an easy website to navigate.
